## Career Progression Framework

The PM career follows three milestones:
- Senior individual contributor PM (median: 3 years to reach)
- Manager of PMs / Group PM (1–2 years at senior IC)
- Manager of PM managers / Director (2–3 years)

Accelerating the first transition — from PM to Senior PM — has the greatest impact on overall career velocity.

---

## The Three Critical Differentiators for Senior PM

### 1. Strategy

Senior PMs are responsible for developing strategies that drive meaningful customer and business success. They paint compelling future visions and chart optimal paths forward while securing executive alignment and resources.

Key distinction: newer PMs prioritize incremental value delivery, while senior PMs align work with long-term vision achievement.

Three components of a complete strategy:

**Vision**: Sketch customer storyboards showing life improvements through your future product. Model infomercial-style narratives highlighting current pain points and how they get resolved.

**Strategic Framework**: Document target markets, pain points, and differentiating bets. Compare alternative approaches with reasoned comparisons — not just listing options but explaining why you're betting on one.

**Roadmap**: Plan multi-year work grouped into strategic themes. Answer "why?" for each component to build frameworks, not just timelines.

### 2. Autonomy

Senior PMs operate independently while building trust through proactive communication and proven delivery. They question handed direction rather than treating it as fixed.

Manager Communication Template that demonstrates senior-level thinking:
1. "Here's a challenge I encountered..."
2. "Here's my proposed approach..."
3. "Any thoughts?"

This format demonstrates decision-making capability while inviting feedback. It signals you're not waiting for instructions — you're bringing a point of view.

### 3. Nuance

"The more senior you get, the more you'll face decisions where the right answer is 'it depends.'"

Senior PMs reason through complex tradeoffs systematically without rigid frameworks. They understand cross-functional constraints and don't prioritize their own team's goals over the organization's. They hold multiple valid perspectives simultaneously without needing to resolve them prematurely.

Practice approach: When deciding, identify conditional logic explicitly: "If X is true, then A is best. If Y is true, then B is best." Being able to articulate these conditions is itself a senior-level skill.

---

## What Doesn't Distinguish Senior PMs

Senior PMs don't actually need to be dramatically better than regular PMs at the core day-to-day PM work. The biggest differentiator is strategic scope, not execution quality. A junior PM who executes perfectly on a defined scope is not necessarily ready to be senior — senior requires setting the scope in the first place.

---

## Immediate Action Steps the Article Recommends

- Block calendar time explicitly for strategic work development — it won't happen if it competes with reactive work
- Use the autonomy template in your next manager conversation
- Identify one decision where conditional "it depends" reasoning applies and practice articulating the conditions out loud
