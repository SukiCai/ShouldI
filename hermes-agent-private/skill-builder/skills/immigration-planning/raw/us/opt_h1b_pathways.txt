## The US Immigration Funnel for International Students

Most F-1 students follow the same basic funnel:
  Study → OPT (12 months) → STEM OPT extension (24 months, if eligible) → H-1B lottery → Green card

The problem: every stage has a gate that can close. Many students plan as if each gate is guaranteed — this is the most common and costly mistake.

Critical timing facts:
- OPT application must be filed no more than 90 days before graduation and no later than 60 days after graduation end date — missing this window forfeits the authorization
- STEM OPT extension requires employer to be E-Verify enrolled and student to have a qualifying STEM degree — not all degrees qualify even if the field sounds STEM
- H-1B registration window is typically March 1–18 each year; missing it means waiting another full year
- Cap-gap protection: if your OPT expires while your H-1B is pending, you are covered — but only if your H-1B was filed before your OPT expired

## The H-1B Lottery — What Changed in 2025–2026

As of December 2025, the US ended the purely random H-1B lottery and replaced it with a wage-weighted selection process. Under the new system:

- Positions classified at OEWS Wage Level III or IV receive significantly higher selection probability
- This fundamentally changes strategy: a high-paying offer from a large employer is now worth more than it was before
- A US master's degree or higher gives two lottery entries (advanced degree exemption) — this still applies

Practical implications:
- Students targeting low-wage-level positions (common in nonprofits, early-stage startups, some academic roles) face worse lottery odds than before
- Employers offering above-market salaries now have structural advantage in retaining international talent
- The total annual cap remains 85,000 (65,000 regular + 20,000 advanced degree)

Historical context: In peak years, selection rates were as low as 15–20% even for advanced degree applicants. The new system means odds vary dramatically by wage level and employer.

## If You Don't Get Selected in the H-1B Lottery — 10 Pathways

### 1. Use Remaining OPT / STEM OPT
If you have STEM OPT remaining, you can try the lottery again next year. Many students get H-1B on the second or third attempt. The math: with 3 years of STEM OPT, you have 3 lottery shots.

### 2. Cap-Exempt H-1B Employers
Universities, nonprofits affiliated with universities, and nonprofit/government research organizations can file H-1B petitions year-round without going through the lottery. Trade-off: you must stay with that employer and salary ranges are often lower.

### 3. O-1 Visa (Extraordinary Ability)
Requires documented national or international recognition — publications, awards, significant original contributions, high salary evidence. Processing: 2–6 months. Duration: 3 years, renewable annually. More attainable than most students think for those with strong academic records and publications.

### 4. L-1 Intracompany Transfer
Requires 1 year of employment abroad with the same company in the past 3 years. Most relevant for students who can arrange to work for a foreign subsidiary first, then transfer to the US office. L-1A (managers/executives) allows transition to EB-1C green card — a significant advantage.

### 5. TN Visa (Canada/Mexico nationals only)
Available to Canadian and Mexican citizens in designated professions (engineers, scientists, accountants, etc.). Canadians can apply at the port of entry — no consulate appointment needed. Renewable indefinitely in 3-year increments. Fastest pathway for eligible nationalities.

### 6. E-3 Visa (Australian citizens only)
Annual cap of 10,500 — rarely fills. Available in 2-year increments, indefinitely renewable. Requires specialty occupation. Much lower competition than H-1B.

### 7. EB-1 Direct Green Card
Three pathways: extraordinary ability (no employer needed), outstanding professor/researcher (no PERM required), multinational executive/manager. EB-1 has no PERM labor certification requirement — significantly faster than EB-2/EB-3 for most countries. For Chinese and Indian nationals, EB-1A may still face backlogs, but EB-1 is faster than EB-2.

### 8. Pursue an Advanced Degree
Enrolling in a US master's or PhD program resets OPT eligibility and gives access to the 20,000-slot advanced degree pool. Trade-off: cost, 1–5 years of time, opportunity cost of income.

### 9. J-1 Exchange Visitor
Available for professors, researchers, trainees, interns. Warning: many J-1 categories impose a 2-year home residency requirement before switching to H-1B or green card status — check carefully before accepting.

### 10. Work Abroad While Pursuing US Status
Work for 1 year outside the US at a multinational company, then transfer on L-1. Or work in Canada, build points for Express Entry, and enter Canada while reapplying for US status later.

## What Most Students Get Wrong About H-1B Strategy

**Mistake 1: Treating H-1B as automatic**
Many students assume "my employer will figure it out." Employers don't always know the lottery odds or have immigration counsel. Students should ask employers directly about their H-1B sponsorship history and success rate.

**Mistake 2: Not understanding STEM OPT eligibility**
Not all STEM-sounding degrees qualify. The degree must appear on the DHS STEM designated degree program list. This should be verified before graduation, not after.

**Mistake 3: Ignoring cap-exempt employers**
Universities, research labs, and certain nonprofits file H-1B without lottery. Many students dismiss academic paths without realizing they allow stable immigration status while building credentials for future O-1 or EB-1.

**Mistake 4: Not using the wage-weighted lottery to their advantage**
Under the new system, negotiating a higher salary (to reach Wage Level III or IV) now directly improves lottery odds. Students leave both money and immigration probability on the table by not negotiating.

**Mistake 5: Treating OPT expiration as a hard deadline**
Cap-gap protection and various bridge mechanisms exist. An attorney consultation 6 months before OPT expiration is essential, not optional.

## Key Diagnostic Questions for US Immigration Path

1. Is your degree on the DHS STEM designated degree program list? (determines STEM OPT eligibility)
2. Has your employer used H-1B sponsorship before, and what's their approval rate? (signals institutional readiness)
3. What wage level would your position be classified at under OEWS? (determines lottery odds under new system)
4. Does your employer have cap-exempt status (university, nonprofit research)? (determines if lottery is even necessary)
5. What is your nationality, and do you have TN or E-3 eligibility? (may make H-1B unnecessary)
6. Do you have publications, awards, or other extraordinary ability documentation? (O-1 pathway assessment)
7. Does any multinational company you'd consider working for have offices in your home country? (L-1 pathway assessment)
