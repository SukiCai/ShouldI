# EB-2 Green Card: Employment-Based Second Preference

## Three Eligibility Categories

1. Advanced Degree: requires a master's or higher (or foreign equivalent), OR a bachelor's degree plus 5 years of progressive work experience in the field
2. Exceptional Ability: must demonstrate expertise "significantly above that ordinarily encountered in the sciences, arts, or business" — typically requires 3 of 6 evidence types
3. National Interest Waiver (NIW): self-petition without employer sponsor by proving work has substantial merit, national importance, and that waiving labor certification is in US national interest

## PERM Labor Certification Process (Required for EB-2 Advanced Degree / Exceptional Ability)

Before filing Form I-140, employers must obtain PERM certification from DOL:
1. Prevailing wage determination (employer files for DOL determination)
2. Pre-filing recruitment (two Sunday newspaper ads, 30+ days, state workforce job posting)
3. Waiting period (60 days after last recruitment activity)
4. File Form ETA-9089

Timeline: ~6 months pre-filing recruitment + ~158 days DOL processing = approximately 1 year total for PERM alone.

NIW (National Interest Waiver) bypasses PERM entirely — this is its primary advantage.

## Form I-140 Processing
- Standard processing: ~8.1 months (as of 2025)
- Premium processing (I-907): 15 business days, fee ~$2,805
- Employer pays I-140 fee: $715

## Priority Dates and Backlogs — The Hidden Crisis
Priority date = date PERM application was filed (or I-140 for NIW). Must be "current" for visa to be issued.

For India-born applicants: wait times in EB-2 and EB-3 can be 50-100+ YEARS due to per-country quotas (7% cap per country regardless of demand). India and China face extreme retrogression.

For most other countries: EB-2 is typically current (no wait) or only a few months behind.

EB-2 to EB-3 downgrade strategy: India-born applicants sometimes downgrade from EB-2 to EB-3 when EB-3 priority dates advance faster than EB-2 — this allows them to get a green card sooner. This is a legal strategy but requires consultation with an immigration attorney.

## National Interest Waiver (NIW) — Self-Petition Track

Three-prong test (Matter of Dhanasar, 2016):
1. The proposed endeavor has substantial merit and national importance
2. The applicant is well-positioned to advance the proposed endeavor
3. On balance, it would be beneficial to the United States to waive the job offer and labor certification requirements

Who typically qualifies:
- STEM researchers (PhD-level, publications, citations, peer review experience)
- Healthcare professionals in underserved areas
- Entrepreneurs with significant US economic impact
- Policy experts, educators in critical fields

NIW approval rate: 54% (per Q3 2025 USCIS data), down 13 percentage points — increased scrutiny.
Backlog for NIW decisions: nearly doubled to 4.3 months pending.

Evidence types for NIW:
- Citations and publications in peer-reviewed journals
- Patents
- Membership in professional associations requiring outstanding achievement
- Critical role at distinguished organizations
- High salary relative to peers in field
- Media coverage

## Total Costs (as of August 2025)
Filing within US:
- Form I-140: $715 (employer) + $235 immigrant fee
- Form I-485 (adjustment of status): $1,440

Filing from outside US:
- Form I-140: $715
- DS-260: $345
- Form I-864: $120

## H-1B to Green Card Connection
H-1B holders pursuing EB-2/EB-3 can file I-140 while on H-1B. Once I-140 is approved and priority date is current, they file I-485 (if in US) or go through consular processing. H-1B status can be extended in 3-year increments once I-140 is approved, even past the normal 6-year cap (AC21 Section 104(c)).

## Key Strategic Insight
For Indian and Chinese nationals: the per-country quota backlog is the dominant variable — not processing time or qualifications. A highly qualified Indian EB-2 applicant may wait longer than a much less qualified candidate from an undersubscribed country. Planning for US permanent residence as an Indian national requires accepting that this is a decade-scale commitment under current rules.
