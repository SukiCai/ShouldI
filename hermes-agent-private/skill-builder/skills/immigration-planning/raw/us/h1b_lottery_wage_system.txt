# H-1B Visa: Cap, Lottery, and Wage-Based Selection System

## Annual Cap Numbers
- Regular cap: 65,000 visas per fiscal year
- Advanced degree exemption (master's cap): additional 20,000 for US master's degree or higher
- Total available: 85,000 per fiscal year

## FY 2027 Registration Timeline
- Registration window: March 4–19, 2026 (14-day window)
- Selection announcements: by March 31, 2026
- Petition filing window: April 1–June 30, 2026 (90-day window)
- Earliest employment start date: October 1, 2026
- Registration fee: $215 per beneficiary (non-refundable)

## Major Change: Wage-Based Selection System (Effective FY 2027)

DHS finalized a weighted entry system replacing pure random selection. Final rule effective February 27, 2026. Each registration receives entries based on offered salary vs. DOL prevailing wage levels:

| Wage Level | Entries in Lottery |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Level IV (highest, typically senior/expert) | 4 entries |
| Level III (experienced) | 3 entries |
| Level II (qualified) | 2 entries |
| Level I (entry-level) | 1 entry |

Key nuance: Registration wage level (OEWS-based) and LCA wage level can differ. Employers register at the highest OEWS level the salary meets, even if the actual job is classified lower by DOL. An entry-level role can be registered at Level III or IV if the offered salary is high enough.

## Rationale for Change
DHS stated the previous random system "was exploited and abused by U.S. employers who were primarily seeking to import foreign workers at lower wages than they would pay American workers."

## Beneficiary-Centric Rule
Each unique individual enters the lottery only once, even if multiple employers submit registrations for that person. This prevents lottery stacking abuse.

## Registration Statuses
- Submitted: eligible for selection
- Selected: employer may file Form I-129
- Not Selected: not chosen (does not affect future year eligibility)
- Denied: duplicate submissions from same employer invalidated
- Invalidated – Failed Payment: payment unsuccessful

## Cap-Exempt Employers (No Lottery Required)
- Universities and colleges
- Nonprofit research organizations affiliated with universities
- Government research organizations
- Certain nonprofits and healthcare institutions

Cap-exempt employers can hire H-1B workers at any time of year without going through the lottery.

## Post-Selection Steps
If selected: file Form I-129 (Petition for Nonimmigrant Worker) during the April–June window. File at the location specified in the selection notice.

If not selected: registration may remain eligible for additional rounds if cap not filled. Alternative visa paths to consider: L-1 (intracompany transferee), O-1 (extraordinary ability), TN (Canada/Mexico nationals), E-3 (Australian nationals), J-1 (exchange visitor).

## Impact on OPT/STEM OPT Workers
Workers already in the US on OPT with higher salary offers benefit most from wage-based system. If total registrations decline 50%+ as projected, selection rates for higher-wage positions could approach 50–90%.

## Employer Common Mistakes
- Missing the 14-day registration window (no exceptions)
- Submitting duplicate registrations for same beneficiary (results in denial)
- Filing Form I-129 at incorrect USCIS location
- Failing to maintain accurate wage documentation

## Strategic Implications
- Higher salary positioning directly increases H-1B selection odds
- Employers who pay prevailing wages at Level III–IV get 3–4x more entries
- For entry-level hires, employers can still register at higher OEWS levels if the salary offered is high enough — the OEWS level is determined by the salary, not the job title
- Early career international students on OPT should negotiate higher starting salaries, as this directly affects H-1B odds, not just immediate income
