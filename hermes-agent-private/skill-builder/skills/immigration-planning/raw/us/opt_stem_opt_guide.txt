# OPT and STEM OPT: Complete Guide for F-1 International Students

## What is OPT
Optional Practical Training (OPT) allows F-1 student visa holders to work in the US in jobs directly related to their degree. Does not require employer sponsorship, reducing hiring barriers.

Standard duration: 12 months of work authorization.

## Pre-Completion vs Post-Completion OPT
- Pre-completion OPT: work before graduating, counts against the 12-month total
- Post-completion OPT: work after graduating, most common path
- Most international students pursue post-completion OPT for the full 12 months

## Application Timeline (Critical — Missing Deadlines = Denial)
- Earliest to apply: 90 days before program end date
- Latest to file I-765 with USCIS: 60 days after program end date
- After DSO recommends OPT in SEVIS: must submit Form I-765 within 30 days — missing this 30-day window results in automatic denial
- USCIS processing: 2–5 months (premium processing available)
- Recommended: apply as early as possible (90 days before graduation)

## STEM OPT 24-Month Extension

Students with qualifying STEM degrees can extend OPT by 24 additional months (total: 36 months).

Eligibility requirements:
1. Currently in valid period of post-completion OPT
2. Earned bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree from accredited US institution
3. Degree's CIP code appears on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program list
4. Job offer from employer enrolled in E-Verify program (mandatory)

## Unemployment Limits (Strictly Enforced)
- Regular post-completion OPT: maximum 90 days of unemployment allowed
- STEM OPT extension period: maximum 150 days total (not 150 additional — cumulative)
- Exceeding these limits terminates F-1 status automatically
- Students must actively track their own unemployment days

## E-Verify Requirement for STEM OPT
STEM OPT employers must be enrolled in E-Verify — no exceptions. Regular OPT has no E-Verify requirement.

## Changing Employers During STEM OPT
- Can change employers as long as new position directly relates to your degree field
- Must submit a new Form I-983 Training Plan for each new employer
- New employer must also be enrolled in E-Verify
- Report employer change to DSO within 10 days

## Form I-983 Training Plan Requirements
- Must explain how the job duties relate to the STEM degree
- Must be signed by both student and employer supervisor
- Incomplete or vague descriptions lead to DHS audit scrutiny
- USCIS has increased audits on STEM OPT I-983 documentation compliance

## Reporting Obligations (10-Day Rule)
Students must report within 10 days (via SEVP Portal or DSO):
- Changes to physical or mailing address
- Legal name changes
- Employer information changes
- Plans to transfer schools or travel internationally

## Cap-Gap Bridge (OPT to H-1B)
If an H-1B petition is filed and pending for a student whose OPT is expiring, cap-gap automatically extends F-1 status and work authorization until October 1 (H-1B start date). This is automatic — no separate filing needed.

## Strategic Considerations for International Students
1. Choose STEM-designated programs if available — the 24-month extension is highly valuable
2. Do NOT delay OPT application — processing delays can cause gaps in work authorization
3. Track unemployment days carefully from day one
4. Negotiate higher starting salary — salary level now directly affects H-1B lottery odds (wage-based system FY2027+)
5. Employer must be E-Verify enrolled for STEM OPT — confirm this before accepting job offer
6. Use STEM OPT period to build skills and compensation for stronger H-1B registration

## Common Mistakes
- Applying for OPT too late (less than 90 days before graduation)
- Missing the 30-day I-765 filing window after DSO recommendation
- Accepting STEM OPT job at non-E-Verify employer
- Exceeding 90-day unemployment limit without noticing
- Submitting vague Form I-983 that doesn't clearly connect job to degree
- Failing to report employer/address changes within 10 days
