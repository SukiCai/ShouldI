# Canadian Experience Class (CEC): PR Pathway for International Students

## Core Eligibility Requirements

Three mandatory criteria:

1. Work Experience: at least 1 year of skilled, professional or technical work in Canada within 36 months of application date
   - 1 year = minimum 1,560 hours
   - Can be achieved: full-time (30 hrs/week × 52 weeks) OR equivalent part-time
   - Must be in TEER categories 0, 1, 2, or 3 (managerial, professional, technical, skilled)

2. Language Proficiency:
   - TEER 0 or 1 jobs: CLB 7 minimum in all four skills (reading, writing, listening, speaking)
   - TEER 2 or 3 jobs: CLB 5 minimum in all four skills

3. Residency Intent: plan to live and work outside Quebec (Quebec has separate immigration system)

## What Does NOT Count as CEC-Eligible Experience
- Self-employment (not eligible, ever)
- Work experience gained while a full-time student (co-op work terms do NOT count)
- Work exceeding 30 hours/week cannot be counted beyond the 1,560-hour threshold
- Work in TEER 4 or 5 occupations (manual labor, elementary positions)

## Processing
- Express Entry integration: submit profile to pool, wait for ITA based on CRS score
- Processing after ITA: typically within 6 months
- No settlement funds required (unlike FSW) — unique advantage of CEC

## Strategic Advantage for International Graduates

International graduates in Canada follow this path:
1. Complete degree → get PGWP
2. Get TEER 0/1/2/3 job → accumulate work hours
3. After 1 year (1,560 hours): CEC eligible
4. Build CRS score through Canadian experience points + language + age
5. Submit Express Entry profile for CEC
6. If CRS score too low: pursue PNP (provincial nomination = +600 CRS points)

Canadian work experience adds directly to CRS score:
- 1 year Canadian experience: 40 CRS points
- 2+ years: 53 points
- 3+ years: 64 points
- 4+ years: 72 points
- 5+ years: 80 points

## CEC vs. FSW for International Students
Most international graduates use CEC rather than FSW because:
- Foreign work experience before studying in Canada may not qualify or may be harder to document
- Canadian experience gained on PGWP is more easily verified
- CEC doesn't require LMIA or job offer for the PR application (job offer only helps CRS, but March 2025 change removed job offer points)

## Common Timeline for International Student to PR via CEC
- Year 0: Graduate, apply for PGWP
- Year 0–1: Start working in Canada (TEER 0/1/2/3 occupation)
- Year 1: Become CEC eligible, create Express Entry profile
- Year 1–2: Wait for ITA (or pursue PNP for lower CRS candidates)
- Year 2–2.5: PR approved

Total: typically 2–3 years post-graduation if CRS is competitive.
