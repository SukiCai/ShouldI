# Canada Express Entry: How the System Works and CRS Scoring

## What is Express Entry
Electronic management system where eligible candidates submit profiles to a pool. Government ranks candidates using the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) and issues Invitations to Apply (ITAs) in regular draws. Processing target: 6 months after ITA.

## Three Program Streams

### Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)
- 1 year of continuous full-time skilled work in past 10 years
- Language: intermediate ability (CLB 7 minimum in English/French)
- Education: relevant credential with assessment
- Minimum 67 points on FSWP grid (separate from CRS)
- No requirement for Canadian experience

### Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
- At least 1 year of skilled work in Canada within past 36 months (1,560 hours)
- Language: CLB 7 for TEER 0–1 jobs, CLB 5 for TEER 2–3 jobs
- Must intend to live outside Quebec
- Co-op work terms and self-employment do NOT count
- Strategic advantage: international students on PGWP can accumulate CEC-eligible experience

### Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)
- 2 years full-time skilled trades experience within past 5 years
- Job offer for 1 year OR certificate of qualification
- Language: CLB 5 for speaking/listening, CLB 4 for reading/writing

## CRS Score Structure (Total: 1,200 points possible)

### Without Spouse/Partner
- Core human capital: max 500 points
- Skill transferability: max 100 points
- Additional factors: up to 600 points

### With Spouse/Partner
- Principal applicant core factors: max 460 points
- Spouse core factors: max 40 points
- Skill transferability: max 100 points
- Additional factors: up to 600 points

### Core Human Capital Factors
- Age: 0–110 points (maximum at ages 20–29 without spouse)
- Education: 0–150 points (PhD = 150 without spouse)
- Primary language (English/French): up to 136 points without spouse (34 per skill at CLB 10+)
- Secondary language: up to 24 points without spouse
- Canadian work experience: 0–80 points (40 for 1 year, 80 for 5+ years)

### Additional Factors (up to 600 points)
- Provincial Nomination (enhanced): 600 points — virtually guarantees ITA
- Canadian study: 15–30 points (depending on program length)
- French language: 25 additional points (CLB 7+ with lower English) or 50 points (CLB 7+ with CLB 5+ English)
- Sibling in Canada: 15 points
- Arranged employment (LMIA-based or exempt): 50–200 points (NOTE: as of March 25, 2025, arranged employment points removed from CRS)

### Critical 2025 Change
As of March 25, 2025, points for valid job offers have been REMOVED from the Comprehensive Ranking System. Previously, a qualifying job offer could add 50–200 points. This change significantly reduced the advantage of having a Canadian job offer for Express Entry purposes.

## Draw Types
1. General rounds: highest CRS scores across all three programs
2. Program-specific rounds: for specific streams (e.g., FSW only)
3. Category-based rounds: targeting specific economic goals (introduced post-2023) — draws for specific occupations or attributes (e.g., healthcare workers, STEM, trades, French language)

## Recent CRS Cut-Off Trends
Category-based draws often have lower CRS cut-offs than general draws, creating opportunities for candidates with lower scores in targeted fields.

## Skill Transferability Points (max 100)
Awarded for combinations of education + experience + language proficiency. Highest tier: 50 points per category combination.

## Strategic Implications
1. Canadian work experience is disproportionately valuable — adds both CRS points AND CEC eligibility
2. French language skills provide significant advantage (50 extra points + access to French-stream draws)
3. Provincial Nomination gives 600-point boost — making it the most powerful factor after core profile strength
4. Job offer no longer adds CRS points (March 2025) — changes strategic calculus for PGWP holders
5. Category-based draws create opportunities for lower-CRS candidates in targeted sectors
