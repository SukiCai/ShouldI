# Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP): 2024-2025 Rules and Changes

## What is PGWP
Open work permit issued to international graduates of eligible Canadian institutions. Allows working for any employer in Canada. Key bridge from student status to Canadian work experience needed for PR (CEC pathway).

## Duration Rules
- Program 8 months or less: PGWP duration = program length
- Program 8 months to 2 years: PGWP = program length
- Program 2+ years: PGWP = 3 years (maximum)
- If multiple programs: PGWP based on combined length if consecutive with no gap

## November 1, 2024: New Field of Study Requirement

Before November 1, 2024: all PGWP-eligible institution graduates could get a PGWP.
After November 1, 2024: non-degree program graduates must study in an eligible field.

### Who is EXEMPT from field-of-study requirement:
- University bachelor's degree graduates
- University master's degree graduates
- University PhD/doctoral degree graduates
(All exempt — only need to meet language requirement, not field of study)

### Who MUST meet field-of-study requirement:
- College diploma graduates
- College certificate graduates
- Associate degree graduates
- Graduate certificates/diplomas (not university master's)
- Trade/apprenticeship programs

### The Five Originally Eligible Fields (November 2024)
1. Healthcare
2. Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM)
3. Trades
4. Transport
5. Agriculture and agri-food

## June 2025 Major Overhaul: Fields Added and Removed

On June 25, 2025, IRCC overhauled the eligible field list:
- 119 fields newly added
- 178 fields removed
- Total eligible fields: 920 programs

### Newly Added
- Healthcare & Social Services: veterinary medicine, dental studies, clinical sciences
- Education: teacher education programs in French language arts, biology, computer science, chemistry, drama (entire new category)
- Trades: construction management, CNC machinist technology, professional diving instruction
- STEM: architecture and landscape architecture programs

### Removed
- Transport: ENTIRE category removed (airframe mechanics, engine machinist, marine maintenance technician)
- Agriculture: nearly all programs removed (one program remains)
- Environmental Studies: natural resources management, environmental studies

## Language Requirements (New as of November 1, 2024)
- University graduates (bachelor's, master's, PhD): CLB 7 / NCLC 7 in all four abilities
- College/other programs: CLB 5 / NCLC 5 in all four abilities
- Previously no language requirement for PGWP

## Grandfathering Rules
Students who submitted study permit BEFORE November 1, 2024: eligible under old rules regardless of field.
Students who submitted study permit BEFORE June 25, 2025: eligible under rules in effect at their application date, even if their program was removed in the June 2025 overhaul.

## CIP Code — Critical Technical Detail
PGWP eligibility for non-degree programs is determined by the program's Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) code, not the program name. Submitting incorrect CIP codes constitutes misrepresentation — consequences include refusal, fines, and 5-year immigration ban.

## Strategic Implications for International Students
1. University degree (bachelor's/master's/PhD) = PGWP regardless of field — the safest path
2. College programs: verify your specific program's CIP code against IRCC's eligible list BEFORE applying to the school
3. Transport and most agriculture programs lost PGWP eligibility in June 2025 — a major blow for those fields
4. Language testing (IELTS/CELPIP/TEF) now mandatory — factor this into timeline
5. PGWP length = 3 years for 2+ year programs — worth maximizing program duration for maximum PGWP
6. PGWP is the gateway to CEC-eligible work experience → Express Entry PR

## Common Misconceptions
- "Any Canadian college gives PGWP": FALSE since November 2024 — field of study matters for non-degree programs
- "My program is on the eligible list so I'm fine": CHECK again after June 2025 overhaul — 178 programs were removed
- "I have a job offer so I'm guaranteed PR": FALSE — job offer no longer adds CRS points as of March 2025
