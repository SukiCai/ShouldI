# Chapter 1: F-1 Status Maintenance & Enrollment Requirements

## Core Idea
Maintaining F-1 immigration status is entirely the student's responsibility — not the school's. The single most common way students lose status is falling below full-time enrollment without proper authorization. The consequences (deportation, bars to re-entry, reinstatement applications to USCIS) are severe and not automatically reversible.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Full-Time Enrollment Requirement**: F-1 students must carry a full course load every semester except summer.
  - When to use: every registration period
  - How: Undergrad = 12+ credit hours; Grad Master's = 9–12 (school-dependent); Doctoral = 7+ credit hours; English Language Programs = 18+ hours/week
  - Exception: final semester (fewer credits if it's all that's needed to graduate)

- **Reduced Course Load (RCL) — The Only 4 Exceptions**: Only these situations legally allow dropping below full-time. DSOs have NO discretion to create new exceptions.
  1. Academic difficulty (initial English difficulty, unfamiliar US teaching methods, improper course placement)
  2. Medical condition (requires licensed doctor/psychologist documentation; limited to 12 months per academic level)
  3. Final semester (fewer courses needed to complete degree)
  4. Improper course level placement
  - When to use: only when one of the 4 applies
  - How: student MUST obtain DSO approval BEFORE dropping courses — retroactive approval is not permitted

- **Out-of-Status and Reinstatement**: Student who falls out of status must apply to USCIS for reinstatement.
  - 5-month rule: must not have been out of status for more than 5 months (or must show exceptional circumstances)
  - Cannot have a record of repeated or willful violations
  - Student must be pursuing or intending to pursue full course of study

## Key Concepts
- **SEVIS**: Student and Exchange Visitor Information System — the federal database tracking all F-1/J-1 students; DSO reports all status changes here
- **DSO (Designated School Official)**: The school official responsible for SEVIS reporting and authorizing RCL, CPT, OPT
- **I-20 Certificate of Eligibility**: The document that authorizes F-1 status; must be signed and current
- **Out-of-Status**: When a student violates any F-1 regulation — this is not automatically fixable; requires USCIS reinstatement application
- **Grace Period (F-1)**: 60 days after program completion to depart, transfer, apply for OPT, or change status
- **Annual Vacation**: Summer — F-1 students are considered in status during summer break even without enrollment; must plan to enroll full-time in fall

## Mental Models
- Think of F-1 status as a set of rules where breaking ONE rule = losing ALL status. There's no minor violation.
- Online courses: F-1 students can only count ONE 3-credit online course toward full-time load per semester. More online courses are allowed BUT don't count toward the 12-credit minimum.
- Concurrent enrollment at two SEVIS-approved schools is permitted — only the primary school (degree-granting) handles SEVIS reporting.

## Anti-patterns
- **Dropping below full-time without DSO approval**: Most common status violation. Students assume they can fix it retroactively — they cannot.
- **Using summer vacation logic in non-summer terms**: Annual vacation only applies to summer; students cannot skip fall/spring terms.
- **Assuming medical hardship is automatic**: Medical RCL requires official documentation from a licensed provider and DSO approval before dropping courses.
- **Out-of-status for more than 5 months before seeking reinstatement**: After 5 months, reinstatement becomes much harder (requires showing exceptional circumstances).

## Worked Example
Student Jane (F-1, junior undergrad) gets hospitalized mid-semester and cannot maintain 12 credits. **Wrong**: drops to 6 credits without telling DSO. **Right**: contacts DSO immediately, provides doctor documentation, DSO authorizes Medical RCL in SEVIS before the course drop. Jane stays in status. The difference: one conversation with the DSO made before dropping courses.

## Key Takeaways
1. F-1 full-time enrollment is a federal requirement, not a school preference — dropping below it without DSO approval = out of status immediately
2. Only 4 RCL exceptions exist; there is no "common sense" or hardship exception outside these 4
3. DSO approval must be obtained BEFORE dropping below full-time, not after
4. Out-of-status students have at most 5 months to seek reinstatement before the process becomes significantly harder
5. Summer break is the only break where non-enrollment is automatically permitted
