# Chapter 2: Work Authorization — On-Campus, CPT, and OPT

## Core Idea
F-1 students have three legitimate work authorization pathways in sequence: on-campus work (incident to status), CPT during studies, and OPT after graduation. Each has strict eligibility conditions, and unauthorized employment — even one day — is a deportable offense that ends any pathway to H-1B or green card.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **On-Campus Employment (Incident to Status)**
  - No special authorization needed for F-1; J-1 requires written RO authorization
  - Limit: 20 hours/week while school is in session; full-time during official breaks
  - CUNY policy: includes all CUNY campuses system-wide
  - Cannot start more than 30 days before classes begin (initial entry)

- **CPT (Curricular Practical Training) — 8 CFR 214.2(f)(10)(i)**
  - When to use: internship/co-op that is an integral, required part of the degree program
  - Eligibility: 1 full academic year of lawful full-time enrollment + job offer + DSO authorization + registered course that awards credit
  - CRITICAL: Full-time CPT for 12+ cumulative months = permanently ineligible for OPT. Part-time CPT does not affect OPT eligibility.
  - Exception to 1-year rule: graduate programs that require immediate CPT participation as part of the curriculum
  - Authorization: DSO must sign I-20 BEFORE employment begins

- **OPT (Optional Practical Training) — 8 CFR 214.2(f)(10)(ii)**
  - Pre-completion OPT: available before graduation; part-time (20 hrs/week while school is in session); deducted from 12-month OPT at half rate (6 months part-time pre-OPT = 3 months deducted from post-OPT)
  - Post-completion OPT: 12 months; employment must begin within 90 days of OPT start date
  - STEM OPT Extension: additional 17-24 months for STEM degree holders; requires E-Verify employer

- **Severe Economic Hardship Employment — 8 CFR 214.2(f)(9)(ii)**
  - Last resort off-campus option; not related to field of study
  - Requirements: 1 full academic year in F-1 status + documented severe unforeseen hardship + USCIS approval (EAD required)

- **J-1 Academic Training (AT)**
  - Equivalent of OPT for J-1 students
  - Up to 18 months total; must directly relate to field of study
  - Post-doctoral: up to 36 months (inclusive of prior AT)
  - Must have job offer and RO authorization before starting

## Key Concepts
- **EAD (Employment Authorization Document)**: The physical card issued by USCIS that authorizes OPT work; cannot start working until EAD arrives
- **E-Verify**: Federal system employers use to verify work eligibility; REQUIRED for STEM OPT extension employers
- **Cap-Gap**: Automatic extension of F-1 status and OPT if H-1B petition is filed and pending while OPT expires
- **RO/ARO (Responsible Officer/Alternate RO)**: J-1 equivalent of DSO
- **Unauthorized employment**: Working without proper EAD/authorization; terminates status, bars reinstatement, triggers deportation

## Mental Models
- Use CPT for: required internships during school where the course credit depends on the work
- Use OPT for: post-graduation work authorization, especially to bridge to H-1B
- The 12-month CPT rule is a trap: students who take full-time CPT for a year to earn money lose their OPT eligibility entirely — trading a permanent benefit for a short-term one
- Part-time pre-completion OPT is not free: every 2 months of part-time pre-OPT = 1 month deducted from post-completion OPT

## Anti-patterns
- **Full-time CPT for 12+ months**: Permanently eliminates OPT eligibility. Common with students who do year-long internships for pay without understanding the consequence.
- **Starting work before EAD arrives**: Even one day of unauthorized employment ends F-1 status. Students assume OPT approval notice (I-797C receipt) is sufficient — it is NOT.
- **Employer not E-Verify enrolled for STEM OPT**: STEM extension requires E-Verify enrollment at time of filing. Students accept offers from non-enrolled employers and lose the extension.
- **Missing 90-day employment start deadline**: OPT authorization starts on a specific date; if student doesn't begin working within 90 days, they accumulate unemployment days counting toward the 90-day limit.
- **Unauthorized on-campus work for J-1**: Unlike F-1, J-1 students MUST get written RO authorization before any on-campus work.

## Worked Example
Student Wei (F-1, STEM Master's) takes a full-time internship via CPT in year 2. **Scenario A**: 6 months full-time CPT → still OPT-eligible (full 12 months). **Scenario B**: 13 months full-time CPT → permanently loses OPT. Wei in Scenario B cannot bridge to H-1B via OPT and must find alternative status immediately after graduation. The CPT supervisor told him "it's fine, CPT is unlimited" — technically true for CPT itself, but the 12-month rule kills the OPT option permanently.

## Key Takeaways
1. Unauthorized employment is the single most severe violation — one day terminates status with no reinstatement path
2. Full-time CPT exceeding 12 months permanently eliminates OPT eligibility — this is a permanent, irreversible consequence
3. OPT work cannot begin until the physical EAD card is in hand
4. STEM OPT extension requires E-Verify-enrolled employer — verify this before accepting a job offer
5. J-1 on-campus work requires RO written authorization; F-1 on-campus work does not
