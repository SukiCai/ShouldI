International Student Guide: 

Post Graduate Work Permit Application 

The PGWP application is made online through your secure IRCC account. 

You must apply for the PGWP within 180 days of receiving your letter of completion—
you don’t need to wait for your diploma/certificate or for convocation. 

You must ensure your study permit is valid until at least the completion date of your 
program and when you apply for your PGWP. 

To be eligible for a post-graduation work permit (PGWP), you must meet all eligibility 
requirements. 

General eligibility 

You meet the general eligibility requirement to apply for a PGWP if 

•  you completed a study program at a designated learning institution 
•  you maintained full-time status as a student in Canada during each semester of your study 

program (exception for final term)  

•  you apply for your PGWP within 180 days after you graduate 
•  your study permit was valid at some point during the 180 days after you graduated 

and before you apply for your PGWP 

Language requirements 

As a graduate of a college program, you must demonstrate a minimum CLB 5 on an English language 
proficiency test.  The language tests approved by IRCC are CELPIP General, IELTS General or PTE Core. 
Your test results can be no more than 2 years old. 

Eligibility based on field of study (for all students who applied for their study permit after 
November 1, 2024): 

If you submitted your study permit application on or after November 1, 2024, you must have graduated 
in an eligible field of study to be eligible for a PGWP. When you submit your PGWP application, you will 
need to demonstrate that your program of study is listed in IRCC’s current list of eligible Classification 
of Instructional Program (CIP) codes. NSCC maintains a list of PGWP eligible programs and their 
corresponding CIP codes. These programs are linked to occupations in Canada with a long-term labour 
shortage. The fields of study are currently linked to 5 broad occupational categories:

Getting your Documents from NSCC: 

-  After you have submitted all your course work and/or work placement documents at the end of 

your program, your faculty will put a final grade in for each of your courses or a P for pass for your 
work placement.  

-  The Assistant Registrar at your campus will confirm you have completed the requirements for your 

program.  

-  You will then get your letter of completion emailed to your personal email.  
-  Once you receive your letter of completion, you can request your final transcripts using this form.  

Submitting the application: 

Processing time can vary.   

You may be required to submit biometrics for this application if you have not already done so 
for a previous application.  If you need to provide biometrics, IRCC will notify you after you submit 
your application online.  There is a fee of $85 for this process. 

You may have to provide results from a medical exam performed in the last 12 months, if: 

•  Your work will be in a job where public health must be protected 
•  You lived in a “designated country” for 6 months in a row within the last year  

Find out if you need a medical: https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-
citizenship/services/application/medical-police/medical-exams/requirements-temporary-residents/country-
requirements.html

Working while you wait 

After submitting your PGWP application online, you can work full-time while you wait for a decision if you 
meet this criteria:  

•  Your study permit was valid at the time of your submission 
•  You were enrolled full time in a degree or diploma program that was longer than eight months 
•  You did not work over the authorized number of hours per week when you were a student 

https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/study-canada/work/after-graduation/after-you-
apply.html 

You are not authorized to work after completing your studies if you have not submitted your 
PGWP application.   

If your application for your PGWP is denied, your legal status will end.  If you are employed, you must 
stop working immediately, and you will be asked to leave Canada.  You may be able to apply to restore 
your status as a visitor, depending on your situation. 
https://www.cic.gc.ca/english/helpcentre/answer.asp?qnum=1181 

Travel after submitting an application for PGWP 

You can travel after submitting your application online. However, please note the following:   

•  You must have a valid TRV or eTA to re-enter Canada (if you need to renew your TRV, you won’t 

be able to do this until you have a new status – your PGWP) 

•  The border officer may ask you for proof that you applied for a post-graduation work permit (a 

copy of your application, the fee payment receipt, etc.) 

When you return to Canada: 

•  You may enter as a student if your study permit is still valid 
• 
• 

If IRCC approved your PGWP while you were away, you may enter as a worker 
If your PGWP is still in process, you may enter as a visitor, and you can work without a work 
permit until a decision is made (if you met the criteria to do so) 

https://www.cic.gc.ca/english/helpcentre/answer.asp?qnum=1309
