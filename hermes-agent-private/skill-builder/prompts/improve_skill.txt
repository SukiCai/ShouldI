## Your Task

Produce an improved version of this SKILL.md that addresses the specific weaknesses and gaps identified in the quality report. Follow these rules exactly:

### What to keep
- All content that scored 5/5 in the evaluation — do not weaken or dilute it
- The YAML frontmatter (name, slug, description, domain_description, tags) — unchanged
- The overall structure and expert framing that the evaluator praised in Strengths

### What to improve
Address EVERY item in `weaknesses`, `improvement_suggestions`, and `missing_coverage` from the report. For each one:
- `weaknesses` → fix or expand the specific section causing the weakness
- `improvement_suggestions` → implement the suggestion concretely (add the sub-checklist, the script template, the decision tree, etc.)
- `generic_advice_flags` → replace flagged generic advice with specific, operational guidance (scripts, thresholds, concrete examples)
- `missing_coverage` → add a new section or sub-section for each gap

### How to improve (style rules)
- Expert heuristics must include BOTH the rule AND the reasoning behind it
- Diagnostic questions must be operational — a yes/no or specific answer should change the advice
- Scripts and templates must be concrete skeletons, not vague descriptions
- Statistics must be hedged ("estimates range from X–Y") or sourced; remove false precision
- Every new section must match the voice and density of the best-scoring sections
- Do NOT add padding, summaries of what you changed, or meta-commentary
- Do NOT inflate the skill with generic advice — every sentence must earn its place
- Target length: aim to stay within 15–20% longer than the original. Every added sentence must earn its place. If a weakness can be fixed by rewriting an existing sentence rather than adding a new one, prefer the rewrite. Never add padding to reach a length target.

### Output format
Return ONLY the complete improved SKILL.md content, starting with `---` (the YAML frontmatter). Do not wrap in code fences. Do not add any explanation before or after.
